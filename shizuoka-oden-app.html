Shizuoka Kantodaki

The soul food of Shizuoka — black broth, skewered ingredients, dashi powder & aonori. A one-of-a-kind experience!

📌 This guide is for your reference & enjoyment — to help you understand and appreciate what you are eating. It is not an ordering tool.

What is Kantodaki / Oden?

In Shizuoka, oden is often called 関東煮（かんとだき） — "kantodaki." The dish features a rich dark broth made from beef tendon and pork offal, topped daily using the tsugi-tashi (継ぎ足し) method. Despite the deep color, the flavor is surprisingly mild and savory. Every skewer is finished with dashi powder and aonori.

History

Since the 1950s, over 100 oden stalls lined the streets of central Shizuoka. Workers and students relaxed here after long days. Today, Aoba Oden Street (青葉おでん街) and Aoba Yokocho (青葉横丁) carry on this warm tradition — lanterns glowing, small cozy stalls side by side. Oden is so beloved here it was once sold in dagashiya (candy stores)!

3 Defining Characteristics 🌟

① Black broth — dark soy-based stock, daily replenished, surprisingly light
② Kuro Hanpen (黒はんぺん) — black fish cake unique to Shizuoka, made from whole mackerel & sardine
③ Dashi powder + Aonori — sprinkled on top; this is the essential finishing touch, not optional!
